# On-premises

Connect an existing on-site cluster — your own hardware or a private data center — to Vantage.

## How it works

On-premises clusters connect to Vantage through a lightweight agent deployed on your infrastructure.
The agent runs on your cluster's head node, phones home to Vantage, and from that point your cluster appears in the Clusters list like any cloud cluster.
Jobs submitted through Vantage are scheduled by your existing Slurm scheduler — nothing changes on the cluster side.

## Connect your cluster

On-premises cluster setup requires coordination with Vantage to ensure the agent is deployed correctly for your specific infrastructure.

1. **Create a support task** — Reach out to Vantage support and describe your cluster setup (head node OS, Slurm version, network configuration).
A support engineer will provide the exact agent installation command for your environment.

1. **Install the Vantage agent** — Run the installation command on your cluster's head node.
The agent establishes an outbound connection to Vantage — no inbound firewall rules required.

1. **Watch it connect** — Go to **Compute > Slurm** in Vantage. Your cluster appears and flips to **Connected** once the agent is reporting.

1. **Explore the detail page** — Click the cluster row to open the detail view. The **Nodes**, **Partitions**, **Queue**, and **Configuration** tabs pull live data from your scheduler.

:::tip
The agent only needs outbound HTTPS access to Vantage servers. If your cluster is behind a firewall, check that outbound port 443 is not blocked.
:::
